Sishya (Student): Madhushri Muthuraj

Madhushri started learning Bharatanatyam under the tutelage of Dr. Janaki Rangarajan at age 6. She has been learning Bharatanatyam for 9 years now. She has participated in many annual day functions and performances under Dr. Janaki’s guidance. Madhushri is a rising 10th grader at the Center for Leadership, Government, and Global Economics at Douglas S. Freeman High School. In the future, she wishes to pursue a career in either Law or Engineering. Outside of dance, she has played tennis for 4 years, playing on her school’s team in middle and high school. She also enjoys crocheting and playing the cello. She participated in robotics for 3 years, on a VEX robotics team, making it to the state tournament 2 of the 3 years. In school, Madhushri also participates in numerous extracurriculars, including Model UN and Mock Trial. While learning dance over the past years, Madhushri has learnt numerous skills that have aided her in activities in and out of dance. She has learnt discipline, punctuality, responsibility, respect, and so much more. She wishes to continue learning dance and life lessons from Dr. Janaki in coming years.

Guru (Teacher): Dr. Janaki Rangarajan

Dr. Janaki Rangarajan started learning the South Indian classical dance form, Bharatanatyam, at the tender age of four and since then, has given numerous performances all over India, the United States, Canada, Europe and around the world. Dr. Janaki had the distinction of being the youngest of her Guru’s students to be featured in the television series on the Natyasastra that was aired on India’s national television, Doordarshan. Dr. Janaki has the honor of receiving many prestigious awards including “Vasanthalakshmi - Narasimhachari Award” from Narada Gana Sabha, “Nadanamamani- Shriram Award of Excellence” from Kartik Fine Arts, “Kala Rathna” from Cleveland Thyagaraja Aradhana, “NCA excellence award” from Nungambakkam Cultural Trust, “Singar mani” from Sur Singar Samsad and “Sathya Nrithya Sundaram” from Bharathanjali Trust. She is a graded artist for Doordarshan. She is also an empanelled artist of the Indian Council for Cultural Relations (ICCR). She is an empanelled artist of the Creative Arts Programs (CAPs) of the Arts Council of Fairfax County and has also received the 2010 Strauss Fellowship for “Choreography”.

Orchestra

Sweta Balasubramanian, Vocals

Sweta Balasubramanian hails from the legendary Veena Karaikudi Subbarama Iyer family. She’s a disciple of Vid. Neyveli R Santhanagopalan. Sweta holds a Masters in Music. She’s a B High graded artist of AIR, Chennai. A regular performer in all the sabhas, she holds many awards and accolades for her authentic performances. She’s a dedicated teacher who runs the Samagana School of Carnatic Music, Chennai. She’s currently a faculty of Music at the TNJJMFAU, Adyar.

Sarvesh Karthick, Mridhangam

Sarvesh Karthick is a young proficient Mridhangam artiste, acclaimed as a multifaceted talent. Sarvesh received intensive traditional training on the Mridhangam from Guru Sangita Kala Acharya Sri.T.H.Subash Chandran and his father Kalaimamani Ghatam Dr.S. Karthick. Sarvesh has performed and toured extensively all around India, USA, Singapore and Dubai with many young stars and senior artistes, music directors, reputed dancers and world musicians. Sarvesh is the recipient of numerous awards and titles like - Yuva Kala Bharathi, Laya Ilavarasu, Sangeetha Nakshatra and Sangeetha Mudhra.

T. V. Sukanya, Violin

T. V. Sukanya started her initial training under Shri. Vittal Ramamurthy, Smt. Rekha, Shri A G A GnyanaSundaram, Shri. C N Srinivasa murthy and later received training from Lalgudi.Smt.Vijayalakshmi.
